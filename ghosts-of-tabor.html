Ghosts of Tabor

Ghosts of Tabor is a VR FPS PvPvE survival game that emphasizes hard-core extraction-based gameplay,
requiring players to utilize their wits, skills, and resources to survive in various scenarios such as scavenging, looting, and crafting.

What I did

Ghosts of Tabor is the only extraction based VR FPS PVP and PVE survival game where you will have to use your wits, skills and resources to survive. Inspired by games such as Escape from Tarkov and DayZ, the game features a variety of scenarios from scavenging to looting and crafting. You never know who's around the next corner.

I worked on many things in Ghosts of Tabor including UI/menus, guns, quality of life improvements, status effects, persistent storage areas, and a stable spectator camera to name a few. Many challenges were overcome by communicating with the team and getting the help that I needed when I needed it. Since the game had to run on the Meta Quest 2, I had to always keep performance in mind and optimize my code as much as possible. I wrote original code, as well as debugged and refactored other people's work.

Keep scrolling to learn more about some of the features I worked on.

Streamer Camera System

As part of my work on Ghosts of Tabor, I developed a streamer camera system to enhance broadcast quality and streamers' quality of life. This system allows streamers to effortlessly switch between multiple camera views, zoom in and out on different parts of weapons while in action, enriching the viewer's experience. The system was welcomed by the streamers with great excitement. My role involved integrating the camera system with the game's existing mechanics, focusing on usability and technical performance to support real-time streaming.

Storage Areas and Gun Wall

In Ghosts of Tabor, I engineered a storage system to improve in-game item management and persistence.I designed generic storage areas with clearly defined perimeters to visually indicate where players can save their items reliably. A notable component I developed was the Gun Wall, which allows players to visualize and adjust the placement of firearms. The system provides feedback on invalid placements through material highlights, enhancing user interaction. I also implemented a recursive saving mechanism for custom gun attachments, ensuring that even the most complex configurations are preserved. This save system was later extended to all storage areas within the game, streamlining item management across various contexts.
